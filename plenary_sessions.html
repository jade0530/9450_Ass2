Introduction to Classification ICD-10-AM

Lecturer: Jade Liang

The International Statistical Classification of Diseases and Related Health Problems (ICD) is the foundation of medical statistics. Established by the World Health Organization (WHO), ICD sets the international standard for reporting diseases and health conditions. The Tenth Revision of ICD, Australian Modification (ICD-10-AM) contains classification codes for diseases, injures amd external causes of death. In Australia, ICD-10-AM is utilised in emergency department care in the Australian Emergency Care Classification and subacute episodes of care in the Frailty Related Index of Comorbidities that is used in the Australian National Subacute and Non-Acute Patient Classification.

The latest ICD-10-AM/ACHI/ACS Twelfth Edition is used from 1 July 2022, including not only ICD-10-AM but also Australian Classification of Health Interventions (ACHI) used in classify surgeries and therapies and Australian Coding Standards (ACS), a guideline for application of the above Standards. It is published as a set of five volumes including Tabular lists and alphabetic indexes.

Based on the Independent Health and Aged Care Pricing Authority (IHACPA) learning platform, this session will give you a systematic introduction on ICD-10-AM/ACHI/ACS classification system, and teaches application of utilising the standards in different real life situations.

Utilising HL7 Classification on Health Messaging and Standards

Lecturer: Sally Chen

Health Level Seven International (HL7) is one of the most commonly used methods for standarise electronic health records (EHRs) worldwidely. HL7 was founded by HL7 International in 1987, a non-profit organization established in more tha 50 countries. It is then accredited by the American National Standards Institute in 1994. The standards, formats and definitions provided for medical records is especially useful in medical organizations for instance hospitals and research institutes. HL7 standards support clinical practice and the management, delivery, and evaluation of health services.

HL7 has the following reference categories to provide standards for clincians and organizations:

  • Section 1: Primary Standards
  • Section 2: Clinical and Administrative Domains
  • Section 3: Implementation Guides
  • Section 4: Rules and References

In this session, we will follow the introduction of primary standards provided by HL7 International to learn about the details of primary standards. We will also learn how to classify and verify electronic medical data in various real life situations.
